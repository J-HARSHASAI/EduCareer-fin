BTech in Civil Engineering

Shape the world with innovative infrastructure and sustainable designs

Course Description

Civil Engineering focuses on the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure such as buildings, roads, bridges, and water systems. It equips students with the skills to create sustainable and resilient structures that meet societal needs.

Relevant Subjects

  • Structural Analysis
  • Geotechnical Engineering
  • Transportation Engineering
  • Environmental Engineering
  • Construction Management
  • Hydraulics and Water Resources

Required Skills

  • Structural Design (AutoCAD, STAAD.Pro)
  • Project Management
  • Geotechnical Analysis
  • Surveying Techniques
  • Environmental Impact Assessment
  • Problem Solving
  • Team Collaboration

Job Roles After Completion

Structural Engineer

Job Description

Structural Engineers design and analyze buildings, bridges, and other structures to ensure they are safe, stable, and capable of withstanding environmental loads. They use software like AutoCAD and STAAD.Pro to create detailed designs, perform calculations for load-bearing capacities, and ensure compliance with building codes. They collaborate with architects and construction teams to oversee the implementation of their designs and address any structural issues during construction.

Relevant Subjects

Structural Analysis Construction Management

Required Skills

AutoCAD STAAD.Pro Structural Design Building Codes

Career Path

Junior Structural Engineer Structural Engineer Senior Structural Engineer Structural Consultant

Salary Range

₹6-25 LPA

Industry Demand

High demand in construction, real estate, and infrastructure development due to ongoing urbanization and infrastructure projects.

Companies Providing These Jobs

L&T Construction Tata Projects AECOM Jacobs DLF

Work Environment

Work in offices for design and planning, with frequent site visits to monitor construction progress. Collaboration with architects and contractors is common.
Geotechnical Engineer

Job Description

Geotechnical Engineers analyze soil, rock, and groundwater conditions to ensure the stability of construction projects like foundations, tunnels, and dams. They conduct site investigations, perform tests to assess soil properties, and provide recommendations for foundation designs. They also evaluate risks like landslides or earthquakes and collaborate with project teams to ensure safe construction practices.

Geotechnical Engineering Hydraulics and Water Resources

Transportation Engineer

Job Description

Transportation Engineers plan, design, and maintain transportation systems such as roads, highways, railways, and airports. They analyze traffic patterns, design efficient roadways, and ensure safety and sustainability in transportation infrastructure. They also use software for traffic modeling and collaborate with urban planners to integrate transport systems into city designs.

Construction Project Manager

Job Description

Construction Project Managers oversee the planning, coordination, and execution of construction projects, ensuring they are completed on time, within budget, and to quality standards. They manage resources, coordinate with contractors and suppliers, and ensure compliance with safety and regulatory standards. They also handle project scheduling, budgeting, and risk management using tools like Primavera or MS Project.

Construction Management Structural Analysis

Environmental Engineer

Job Description

Environmental Engineers address environmental challenges in construction projects, focusing on sustainable practices and regulatory compliance. They design systems for waste management, water treatment, and pollution control, and conduct environmental impact assessments for projects. They also ensure compliance with environmental regulations and collaborate with project teams to implement green technologies.
